Lentil Bread

Simple high-fiber lentil bread made entirely from soaked lentils. Great as a dense toast or bread replacement.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dry brown or green lentils, rinsed
  • Water for soaking lentils (enough to cover by 2 inches)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1–2 tablespoons olive oil (optional, for softer texture)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon dried herbs (oregano, thyme, or Italian blend, optional)

Instructions

  1. Rinse lentils and soak 4 hours or overnight in plenty of water.
  2. Drain and rinse. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Prepare a loaf pan.
  3. Blend soaked lentils with salt, baking powder, oil, and seasonings until very smooth.
  4. Pour batter into the loaf pan and smooth the top.
  5. Bake 40–45 minutes, until set and a toothpick comes out mostly clean.
  6. Cool in the pan 15–20 minutes, then cool completely on a rack before slicing.

Notes

  • Adjust seasonings to taste; the base recipe is very flexible.
  • Texture improves after full cooling; it slices much better when cold or room temp.
